Moritz Böhle

I am a PhD student in the Computer Vision and Machine Learning group at the Max Planck Institute for Informatics in Saarbrücken, Germany. I am advised by Prof. Bernt Schiele and Prof. Mario Fritz. My research is focused on interpretable, trustworthy, and responsible deep learning. In particular, over the course of my PhD, I investigated how to design inherently interpretable deep neural networks such as the CoDA and the B-cos Networks, which are optimised to inherently provide explanations that highlight important input features. Before commencing my doctoral studies, I obtained a Bachelor’s degree in Physics at the Free University in Berlin, Germany, in 2016. I went on to study Computational Neuroscience at the Bernstein Center for Computational Neuroscience in Berlin and obtained my Master’s degree in 2019.
